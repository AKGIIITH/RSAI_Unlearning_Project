Problem & Motivation

Why Multimodal Unlearning Matters

Concept-Level Forgetting in VLMs

Current unlearning methods focus on text-only LLMs. Multimodal models (e.g., LLaVA) interleave vision & language — the concept "zebra" lives in both CLIP features and the language decoder. No existing method targets this dual pathway.

No Interpretable Handles

Polysemantic neurons encode multiple concepts. We need a method to isolate monosemantic features tied to a specific concept — this is where Sparse Autoencoders come in.

Retraining is Prohibitive

Fine-tuning or retraining a 7B-parameter VLM from scratch is computationally infeasible. We need inference-time interventions that require zero gradient updates.

Our Approach

Train an SAE on CLIP vision-encoder activations to decompose polysemantic features into sparse, monosemantic latent dimensions. Identify "zebra" features, then apply multiple steering methods: zero-ablation, negative clamping (−k × mean), weight projection, and gradient-based intervention — no full retraining needed.
