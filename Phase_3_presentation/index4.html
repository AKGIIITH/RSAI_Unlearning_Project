Research Objectives & Questions

What We're Investigating

RQ1

SAE Feature Steering in CLIP

Can SAE-based feature steering applied to the CLIP vision encoder produce concept-selective unlearning in a VLM (LLaVA-1.5-7B)?

RQ2

Optimal Intervention Point

Which intervention point — vision encoder, language model, or joint — gives the best forget-vs-retain tradeoff?

RQ3

Language Leakage Problem

Does vision-only clamping leak concept knowledge through the language pathway? (Baseline LL = 96% suggests yes.)

RQ4

Zero-Ablation vs. Steering

How does zero-ablation compare with negative steering (clamping to −k × mean) of concept features?

RQ5

Adversarial Robustness

Is blurred-image robustness reduced more by SAE steering than by gradient-based baselines?

Project Objectives

Train a Sparse Autoencoder on CLIP ViT-L/14 vision encoder activations to decompose features into monosemantic dimensions
Identify concept-specific SAE features for "zebra" via differential activation analysis (zebra − horse)
Build a comprehensive evaluation framework with forget set, retain set, adversarial probes, and text-only leakage probes
Compare multiple steering methods: zero-ablation, negative clamping (−k × mean for k ∈ {5, 10, 20}), SAE-guided weight projection
Evaluate unlearning across 6 metrics: FA, RA, LL, AR to quantify effectiveness and side-effects
